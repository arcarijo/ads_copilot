# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two roles share the platform. The master admin (you) sees everything: every client, every campaign, every strategy — plus user management (passcodes, client assignment) and infrastructure health. Client users are studio and venue owners who log in with a passcode and see only their own businesses: their strategy, their directives (global and per-platform), their campaigns, their reports. One user may own several businesses/ad accounts. These owners have deep audience knowledge and zero marketing training — the product's whole job is translating the first into expert campaigns without requiring the second.

## Product Purpose

An AI co-pilot that runs autonomous Meta ad campaigns for small local businesses — structural setup, daily optimization, performance reporting — while keeping every consequential decision (budget increases, new targeting, strategic direction) gated behind a human. Success is a business owner who trusts the daily report enough to not have to double-check it, and a manager who can run many client accounts without babysitting any single one.

## Positioning

The AI does the disciplined, tireless checklist work a media buyer forgets under pressure — learning-phase patience, kill-loser thresholds, fatigue detection — while every strategic and financial judgment call stays human. The Manager Directive is the literal interface for that boundary: one field where a human's read on the real world overrides the AI's read on the numbers.

## Brand Personality

Bright, cheery, playful, personable — a warm co-pilot in an ocean of sterile corporate platforms. Venue owners appreciate tools with a human feel; this one greets them on a sunny warm-white canvas with coral energy and plain-English coaching, never jargon. Playfulness never becomes toy-like: real ad money is on the line, so numbers stay dark, crisp, and legible under all the warmth. The human-vs-AI dialogue (Manager Directives in plum vs. the Co-Pilot's coral voice) stays the visual throughline.

## Anti-references

Generic dark SaaS — gray-900 surfaces, emerald-500 CTA, flat KPI tiles, no signature. A literal Meta/Facebook-blue clone — this is an independent co-pilot, not a Meta-branded surface. Muted, cautious, apologetic color use that undersells the confidence this product should project.

## Design Principles

Two visible voices, one system: every AI-authored artifact and every human-authored directive stay visually distinct, always. One focal point per screen — campaign/portfolio health leads, everything else is deliberately demoted. Numbers stay legible under any amount of visual energy — boldness never compromises a business owner's ability to read their own spend at a glance. Guardrail logic (budgets, gaps, warnings) gets unambiguous, high-contrast treatment; nothing structurally important should ever hide behind style.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast, ≥3:1 for large text, full keyboard navigation, semantic labels on all interactive elements. No additional stated requirements.
